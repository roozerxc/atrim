# Code of Conduct (or the reason why we don't have such a thing, and we never will)

In the world of free, libre, and open source software (FLOSS), there has been a ton of political discourse between identity politics and the political beliefs of people who work on the software, and people who use the software. The Codes of Conduct of major projects such as Arch Linux, CachyOS, the Linux Kernel and Python have been used and abused to silence feedback and criticism, purely due to the political and ideological interests of the developers not being met.

In other words, responses to such feedback are generally met with scrutiny of varying intensities, so the atrim project will make something strictly clear.

**The atrim project has no plans at all to build a concrete set of guidelines for project contribution.** To put it bluntly, the atrim project is not maintained by people who will police anyone based on political and ideological differences. It gives away the incentive to silence, and dismiss criticism and feedback as "Neo-fascist", "Bigoted", or "containing Hitler particles" which completely does not make any logical sense.

**The atrim project strictly forbids including any ideological and political messaging and/or slogans in the source code, commits, comments, pull requests, and documentation.** For example, the atrim project does not allow the following: "Free Palestine", "Trans Rights", "Anti-Fascist", "Bella Ciao", "Fuck ICE", "Queer-Coded", "Stand with Hong Kong", "Slava Ukraini", "Make America Great Again", "Let's Go Brandon", "RIP Charlie Kirk", etc.

Pull requests that add these will be denied and the contributor (or contributors) will be politely asked to amend their commits with zero ideological/political messages in their code. This is because **it has absolutely nothing to do with the premise of this project**, which is getting Amnesia: The Dark Descent and Amnesia: A Machine for Pigs ported back to the Microsoft Windows 2000 operating system and below minimum spec, with significant modifications and bugfixes.

The atrim project specifically concerns **technical problems**, and is not a platform for activism and political expression.

**As an extension to the above, the atrim project also does not allow off-topic and NSFW contributions.** Issues, pull requests, comments, code, commits and documentation that are inappropriate and have nothing to do with the project's purpose will be immediately rejected with no warning or comment. There is an exception to this, since the base games, Amnesia: The Dark Descent and Amnesia: A Machine for Pigs, may contain nudity, and it is perfectly fine to send screenshot images and video footage of the games in your issue(s).

Additionally, given that this project is based on a game notorious for having one of the most toxic modding scenes, acknowledging this fact helps the atrim project request everyone to stay neutral towards each other regardless of identity or political beliefs. **Neutral meaning no arguing, lashing out, fighting, drama, violence, and vendettas against anyone regardless of the above factors.**

The author of the atrim project welcomes everyone so long as everything stays neutral and within the scope of this project.
